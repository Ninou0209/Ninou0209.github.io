Krakow a beautiful city

Krakow is with 767,348 inhabitants intramural of the second city of Poland after Warsaw. Krakow has always been one of the economic, scientific, cultural and artistic centers of the country. For much of Polish history, it was the capital of the country. That's why she is still in the heart of Poland for the many Poles. Today, Krakow is an important center of local and international tourism, with more than eight million tourists a year. Dating from the 6th century, it is one of the oldest and most important cities in Poland, the architectural heritage is very well preserved. The historic city lies at the foot of Wawel Hill. The historic center of Krakow was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1978. It was the European Capital of Culture in 2000. Mystic city with a hundred churches, with their baroque domes and their golden arrows, old town with its Italian facades, museums, palaces, beautiful Renaissance courtyards and its original buildings, a young and lively city with 70,000 students , lovers of parties and meetings, artistic city where the Viennese tradition of literary cafes continues, Krakow cultivates all styles with happiness.

    • Warsaw the capital

    Deployed on both sides of the Vistula River and capital of Poland since the end of the 16th century, this shining city in the Golden Age was removed from the map in 1945. Carefully reconstructed by the Polish people on the basis of the ancient plans of the medieval order, the unique architectural ensemble of the Old Town (Stare Miasto) with its beautiful Renaissance, Baroque and Gothic frescoes is elevated to World Heritage status by Unesco. Emblem of reconstruction, the Royal Castle where sat the kings of Poland since the eighteenth century, houses some 300 collector's items and sumptuous richly decorated rooms. Warsaw is a city that reconciles the past and the future and marries in the present all the contrasts of a modern and dynamic metropolis, and a theatrical scene alternating with oases of greenery.
